How to Use the Visualization

Select a country and a year from the dropdown menus to update the radar chart. The chart will display the sugar supply, tobacco consumption, and total fat supply for the selected country and year. Hover over the chart to see the exact values along with the life expectancy for the selected parameters.

General Conclusions on Health Factors and Life Expectancy

Examples

Here are some specific examples from the data that illustrate the dependency of life expectancy on the three health factors:

  • Australia (2010): Life Expectancy: 81.70 years, Sugar Supply: 131.0, Tobacco Consumption: 1009.0, Total Fat Supply: 151.3
  • Denmark (2010): Life Expectancy: 79.00 years, Sugar Supply: 120.0, Tobacco Consumption: 1100.0, Total Fat Supply: 140.0
  • France (2010): Life Expectancy: 81.10 years, Sugar Supply: 110.0, Tobacco Consumption: 800.0, Total Fat Supply: 130.0
  • Finland (2010): Life Expectancy: 80.00 years, Sugar Supply: 140.0, Tobacco Consumption: 950.0, Total Fat Supply: 145.0
  • Czechia (2010): Life Expectancy: 76.80 years, Sugar Supply: 160.0, Tobacco Consumption: 1150.0, Total Fat Supply: 160.0

These examples demonstrate that while there are weak negative correlations between these health factors and life expectancy, the relationships are not strong. This suggests that other factors, such as healthcare quality, genetic factors, environmental conditions, socioeconomic status, and lifestyle choices, may also play significant roles in determining life expectancy.
