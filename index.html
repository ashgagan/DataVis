Overview

Analyzing global health disparities and life expectancy factors is crucial for informing policy decisions, effective resource allocation, and raising awareness. Our visualizations help users, including health officials, researchers, and the public, to identify and understand these disparities across countries, highlighting regions with varying life expectancies and potential influencing factors.

Visualization Purpose

The research questions that we aim to answer with our data visualizations are:

  • What are the variations in life expectancy across different OECD countries?
  • Do economic/demographic factors impact life expectancy?
  • How do factors such as tobacco consumption, fat intake, and sugar consumption impact life expectancy?
  • Which countries exhibit the highest and lowest life expectancy, and what are the potential factors contributing to these differences?

The potential benefits of the completed visualizations include:

  • Providing insights into global health inequalities.
  • Facilitating evidence-based policymaking to improve public health outcomes.
  • Educating the public about lifestyle choices and their impact on life expectancy.
  • Identifying regions or countries that may require targeted interventions to improve health outcomes.
